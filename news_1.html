BTC BOATHOUSE 4

Community Outreach Partnership

The International Boatbuilding College Portsmouth have now moved into their permanent home in Boathouse4. A major element of the Boathouse 4 project is to link the dockyard with its community hinterland, providing opportunities for all to experience boatbuilding and ancillary marine skills. We are delighted to be supporting a partnership project where 2 Oarsome-Chance St Ayles Skiffs are being built side by side by community volunteers and school children from Portsmouth.

Led by community participation manager Caroline Barrie Smith and lecturer John Carter 5 local schools and 1 sixth form are sending for weekly structured sessions, 60 young people.In week 4 of the programme the builds are already well advanced and 250 learning hours have been achieved. It is hoped by build completion some 1200-1300 project based vocational skill learning hours will have been achieved.

The fantastic craft skills that these youngsters will have learnt are transportable into numerous professions and will prove to be really useful for their future employment options.

Skiffieworlds 25 to 30 July 2016 Strangford Lough

Oarsome Chance Coastal Rowing project backed Youngsters and Apprentices to enter their community self built St Ayles Skiffs in the Skiffie World Championships in 2016 hosted by the Strangford Lough Partnership.

Strangford Lough, County Down, Northern Ireland, has been selected as the venue for a major, international coastal rowing event, “Skiffieworlds 2016”. 800 competitors, some from as far away as the United States and Tasmania, and up to 60 boats, are expected to participate in these World Championships for the St Ayles’ Skiff class of coastal rowing boat.

The last Skiffieworlds, held in Ullapool, Scotland, attracted entries from Scotland, England, Netherlands, New Zealand, Australia and USA and was attended by around 1000 competitors. Since then several boats have been launched or are in build in other parts of the world including Northern Ireland, Canada, Spain and France.

All will look forward to coming together for celebration, competition and friendship in 2016.
